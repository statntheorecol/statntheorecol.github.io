2023 News

Updates from the current year

  • Matthieu Paquet has now been recruited by CNRS as a permanent researcher. Congrats Matthieu! Matthieu will be based for the foreseeable future in Moulis, Ariège, at the SETE. We’ve got a preprint out with Matthieu which checks whether multispecies Integrated Population Models can output false positives (answer: not unreasonably so) and the framework’s robustness to process stochasticity. Check it out, it is soon-to-be published at PCI/PeerCommunity Journal.

  • Frédéric and Gaël Bardon (MSc student 2020) published a paper on coexistence in stage-structured contexts in Bulletin of Mathematical Biology. The paper shows, among other things, that scenarios where competitive exclusion is predicted in unstructured models allow coexistence in stage-structured models, that it is not related to complex life histories (e.g. with metamorphosis), and that although it works very well for species pairs it is unlikely to be a key driver of species-rich community persistence. We’re following up on this kind of models from a statistical perspective with Matthieu, looking at competition IPMs.

  • We organised in Bordeaux a spring workshop on Integrated Population Models identifiability. A paper will follow, more on that soon-ish.

  • Mélanie Bertrand (2nd year MSc) has been working with us in Spring 2023 on butterfly diversity in (sub)urban environments. She found that butterfly diversity declines with soil sealing levels but not steeply, that prairies have unsurprisingly a positive impact. This has been made easier by a project with i-Sea, who produced a better, high-resolution land-use map of the Bordeaux conurbation in collaboration with us. We now see private gardens and all sorts of green elements, even in the most urbanised areas (see below). Follow-up coming in 2024.

   

   

  • Thomas Besognet (1st year MSc) has been working on replicating a paper by Korpela et al. Proceedings B 2014 on vole population dynamics. We’ve got different results but given what is published at the moment - see this great study by Gould et al., to which participated Matthieu – it should not be too surprising. The reproduction of the vole dynamics paper is not finalized, but please do get in touch if interested.
